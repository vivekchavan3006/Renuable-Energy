          <li>Nacelle ‚Äì The casing on top of the tower that holds the gearbox, generator, and control system.</li>
          <li>Gearbox ‚Äì Increases the rotation speed for the generator.</li>

It works on a simple principle ‚Äî when the wind blows, it moves the turbine blades, which are connected to a rotor. The rotor spins a shaft connected to a generator that produces electricity. Wind turbines are usually mounted on tall towers to capture stronger and more consistent winds. They are made up of several main parts, including the blades, rotor, nacelle, gearbox, generator, and tower. Wind energy is a clean and renewable source of power, as it does not produce harmful emissions or pollution. It helps reduce dependence on fossil fuels and contributes to sustainable energy development. Wind turbines can be installed both on land and offshore, and they are becoming an increasingly important part of modern power generation systems.</li>

      <section id="hydro">
        <div class="feature">
          <h2>Hydroelectric Energy</h2>
          <p class="muted">Hydroelectric systems harness the energy of flowing or falling water to produce electricity. They can supply continuous and reliable energy.</p>
          <h4>üî©Main Components</h4>
          <li>Dam: Stores a large amount of water and creates potential energy.</li>
          <li>Reservoir: Holds the water used for power generation.</li>
          <li>Penstock: A large pipe that carries water from the dam to the turbines.</li>
          <li>Turbine: Rotates when water flows over it, converting energy of water into mechanical energy.</li>
          <li>Generator: Converts mechanical energy from the turbine into electrical energy.</li>
          <li>Powerhouse: Contains the turbines, generators, and control systems.</li>
          <li>Transformer: Increases the voltage of generated electricity for transmission.</li>
          <li>Tailrace: Channel that returns used water to the river.</li>
          <h4>‚öôÔ∏èHow It Works</h4>
          <li>Water stored in a dam or reservoir is released to flow through turbines.</li>
          <li>The flowing water spins the turbine blades.</li>
          <li>The turbine is connected to a generator that converts the mechanical energy into electrical energy.</li>
          <li>The electricity is then sent through power lines to homes and industries.</li>
          <li>After passing through the turbine, the water returns safely to the river.</li>

          <h4>‚ö°Uses of Hydro Power</h4>
          <li>Generating clean and renewable electricity for cities and industries.</li>
          <li>Providing irrigation water and flood control benefits.</li>
          <li>Used in pumped storage plants to balance power supply and demand.</li>
          <li>Supporting rural electrification and remote area development.</li>

          <h4>Advantages:</h4>
            <li>Stable power generation</li>
            <li>Supports grid storage and irrigation</li>
            <li>Low operational costs</li>
            <h4>About</h4>
            <ul class="muted">
            <li>A hydro power plant is a facility that generates electricity from the energy of moving water. It works by storing water in a dam or reservoir and releasing it through large pipes called penstocks toward turbines. The force of the flowing water spins the turbine blades, which are connected to a generator. This generator converts the mechanical energy of the spinning turbine into electrical energy. The electricity is then transmitted through power lines for domestic, industrial, and commercial use.Hydro power is one of the oldest and most reliable sources of renewable energy. It does not produce air pollution or greenhouse gases, making it environmentally friendly. Besides generating power, hydro plants also help in irrigation, flood control, and water supply</li>

  <script>
    // Navigation between sections
    const navLinks = document.querySelectorAll('.nav-link, .cta');
    const sections = document.querySelectorAll('section');

    navLinks.forEach(link => {
      link.addEventListener('click', e => {
        e.preventDefault();
        const target = link.getAttribute('data-target');

        // Highlight current link
        document.querySelectorAll('.nav-link').forEach(l => l.classList.remove('active'));
        const navMatch = document.querySelector('.nav-link[data-target="' + target + '"]');
        if (navMatch) navMatch.classList.add('active');

        // Show corresponding section
        sections.forEach(sec => sec.classList.remove('active'));
        document.getElementById(target).classList.add('active');
        window.scrollTo({ top: 0, behavior: 'smooth' });
      });
    });

    // Solar calculator
    document.getElementById('calc').addEventListener('click', () => {
      const area = parseFloat(document.getElementById('area').value || 0);
      if (area <= 0) {
        document.getElementById('result').textContent = "Please enter a valid area.";
        return;
      }
      const kW = area * 0.15 * 0.8; // 150W/m¬≤ @ 80% efficiency
      document.getElementById('result').textContent =
        `Estimated power generation: ${kW.toFixed(2)} kW (ideal sunlight conditions).`;
    });
